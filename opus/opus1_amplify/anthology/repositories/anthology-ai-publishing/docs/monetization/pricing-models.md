# Pricing Models

## Value-Based Pricing
- Price based on perceived customer value
- Capture more of the consumer surplus
- Align price with benefits delivered
- Challenges:
  - Determining true customer value
  - Value communication to customers
  - Potential for perceived unfairness

## Competitor-Based Pricing
- Price relative to market competitors 
- Simplifies pricing decisions
- Maintains competitive positioning
- Challenges:  
  - Race to the bottom
  - Ignoring own value proposition
  - Reactive instead of proactive

## Cost-Plus Pricing
- Price based on production costs
- Simplest model to implement  
- Ensures profitability
- Challenges:
  - Ignores market forces
  - No incentive for cost reduction
  - Leaves money on the table

## Dynamic Pricing
- Prices fluctuate based on market demand
- Maximizes revenue by exploiting demand variations
- Responds to competition in real-time
- Challenges:
  - Requires robust real-time data
  - Can frustrate customers
  - Potential for price wars

## Subscription Pricing
- Customers pay recurring fee for access
- Predictable revenue streams
- Lowers barriers to adoption
- Challenges:
  - High acquisition costs
  - Ongoing retention challenges
  - Pricing flexibility limitations

## Freemium
- Base product free, charge for premium features
- Attracts large user base
- Allows customer self-segmentation 
- Challenges:
  - Low conversion rates
  - Cannibalization of paid offerings
  - Feature differentiation difficulty
